# The Core Mission: Restoring Agency

This project is a high-transparency substrate for the **Principal Engineer**—those who refuse to be "blinded" by pre-baked configurations and who demand that their editor be an evolving mirror of their own unique mental models.

## The Key Insights (Strategic Pillars)

### 1. From User to Principal
Standard frameworks (LunarVim, LazyVim) turn you into a "User" of someone else’s logic. This project is for the Principal—the one who owns the architecture and views "working states" as hypotheses to be tested, not gifts to be received.

### 2. Radical Observability (High Signal/Low Noise)
We aren't hiding the complexity; we are making the failure points visible. The goal is to optimize the Signal-to-Noise Ratio so that your "Being" can actually perceive the causal links in your code.

### 3. Utility as the Ultimate Filter
We don't ship "features" for the sake of completeness. We ship Successes. If an experiment (like Taskwarrior integration) survives the "Physics" of a high-level workflow, it is shared. If it doesn't, it’s a data point for the Co-architects.

### 4. The "Incomplete" Truth
We reject the "Polished Enclosed Truth." This is a **Living Laboratory**. It is intentionally incomplete because your thinking is evolving. It is a launchpad, not a cage.

## Codebase Preparation: The "Principal" Checklist

Before hitting "Publish" or committing a change, the codebase MUST reflect this philosophy:

- **The "Why" in the Comments:** Don't just show the how. Briefly comment on the intent behind your more radical architectural choices. Let the Co-architects see your reasoning.
- **The "Escape Hatch":** Ensure your structure makes it easy for someone to fork or override your logic. A tool for Principals must be easy to dismantle.
- **The "Telemetry of Failure":** Double-check your error handling. Does the tool tell the user why it failed in a way that helps them understand the system's "Physics"?
- **The Laboratory Folder:** Keep an "Experiments" or "Under Construction" space visible. It signals that this is a work-in-progress journey, not a static product.
