# Premium DX Manifesto: The Principal's Laboratory

## The Core Mission
To restore **Agency** to the engineer. We are building a high-transparency substrate for those who refuse to be "blinded" by pre-baked configurations and who demand that their editor be an evolving mirror of their own unique mental models.

## The Strategic Pillars

### 1. From User to Principal
Standard frameworks (LunarVim, LazyVim) turn you into a "User" of someone else’s logic. This project is for the **Principal**—the one who owns the architecture and views "working states" as hypotheses to be tested, not gifts to be received.

### 2. Radical Observability (High Signal/Low Noise)
We aren't hiding the complexity; we are making the failure points visible. The goal is to optimize the Signal-to-Noise Ratio so that your "Being" can actually perceive the causal links in your code.

### 3. Utility as the Ultimate Filter
We don't ship "features" for the sake of completeness. We ship **Successes**. If an experiment survives the "Physics" of a high-level workflow, it is shared. If it doesn't, it’s a data point for the Co-architects.

### 4. The "Incomplete" Truth
We reject the "Polished Enclosed Truth." This is a **Living Laboratory**. It is intentionally incomplete because your thinking is evolving. It is a launchpad, not a cage.

## 🧪 The Laboratory (Active Experiments)

This is the space for work-in-progress journeys. If you are an agent or collaborator, these are the "Physics" we are currently testing:

### Taskwarrior / Timewarrior Integration
- **Goal:** Exploring seamless terminal and buffer-based workflows to manage tasks and track time directly from the editor without breaking focus.
- **Status:** Under Construction.
- **Current Hypothesis:** A native terminal buffer combined with specific keymaps for `task` and `timew` commands will provide a lower-friction experience than a dedicated plugin.

## 🛠️ The Roadmap

1. **Native-First Core:** Transitioning to Neovim 0.12 `ui2`, native Treesitter, and native Paging.
2. **High-Signal LSP:** Refinement of LSP Hover and Semantic Tokens to ensure structural clarity without highlighter crashes.
3. **Product-Grade Standalone:** Ensuring the configuration is portable, verifiable, and documented as a standalone product.
4. **Modular Skill Distribution:** Decoupling the `development-workflow` skill into a dedicated repository and integrating it via Git submodules to enable cross-project reuse and cleaner dependency management.

## 📋 The "Principal" Checklist
Before you hit "Publish," ensure your codebase reflects this philosophy:

1. **The "Why" in the Comments:** Don't just show the how. Briefly comment on the intent behind your more radical architectural choices.
2. **The "Escape Hatch":** Ensure your structure makes it easy for someone to fork or override your logic.
3. **The "Telemetry of Failure":** Does the tool tell the user why it failed in a way that helps them understand the system's "Physics"?
4. **The Laboratory:** Honor the "Living Laboratory" by keeping experimental logic isolated and visible here in the Manifesto.
